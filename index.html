Process Timing
Notice period (avg 33.9 days) is the single biggest time sink
Key Finding
Timing is surprisingly uniform across departments (44–52 days total). The problem is NOT inter-stage delays — it's the Accept→Join gap (avg 33.9 days) which represents the notice period. Sales takes the longest overall (51.9 days). Customer Support has the slowest Phone→Tech transition (8.0 days vs 7.2 avg).

Compensation Gap Analysis
Gap has no strong effect on offer acceptance — but budget matters
Counterintuitive Finding
Offers below candidates' expectations are NOT significantly less likely to be accepted. Acceptance rates are flat (67–73%) across all gap levels. However, "Salary expectations too high" was cited in 180 rejections — suggesting the issue is companies screening out high-expectation candidates before even making offers.
Offer acceptance rate vs. compensation gap
Gap = (offered − expected) / expected. Negative = offer below expectation.

Recommendations
3 specific, quantified actions
1
Fix the screening bottleneck — review the phone screen criteria
75.2% of applicants (2,255 people) are rejected or ignored before the first screen. This is too aggressive. The most common rejection reasons — "Missing key skills" (224), "Communication concerns" (217), "Culture mismatch" (198) — suggest screeners may be applying overly strict filters. Audit the screening rubric across all 10 departments against Operations (best performer at 4.5% join rate) as the benchmark. Even moving the phone screen rate from 24.8% to 30% would add ~150 candidates to the pipeline.
↑ Est. +30% pipeline volume if screening criteria relaxed by 20%
2
Tackle the notice period crisis — 418 avoidable exits
Half of offer acceptors don't join. "Notice period too long" is the #1 process complaint (182 cases). Solutions: (a) build notice period buyout budgets into offers for high-priority roles, (b) pre-negotiate joining dates at the offer stage — not after acceptance, (c) implement stay-warm programs during the notice period. Design department is worst — only 27.8% of their offer acceptors join. Immediate intervention needed there specifically.
↑ Est. +40 additional joiners per year if Accept→Join rate improves 15 percentage points
3
Audit the Employee Referral program — reallocate to Indeed/Naukri
Employee Referrals produce a 1.6% join rate — worse than all job boards except AngelList. Despite sending 382 candidates, only 6 joined. Meanwhile Indeed (4.2%) and Naukri (3.7%) consistently outperform. Additionally, referrals have the highest withdrawal rate (53 withdrew). Recommendation: (a) reduce referral bonus until quality improves, (b) add quality filters to the referral submission process, (c) redirect budget to Indeed and Naukri campaigns. AngelList should be deprioritised immediately.
↑ Reallocating 50% of referral effort to Indeed could yield ~10 additional joiners per cycle
